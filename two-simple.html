Simple Interest

Before jumping into compound interest, we want to introduce you to simple interests first. Simple interest is easier to learn and also serves as the foundation of compound interest.

Definition

Simple interest is based on the principal amount of a loan or the first deposit in a savings account. Simple interest doesn't compound, which means a creditor will only pay interest on the principal amount and a borrower would never have to pay more interest on the previously accumulated interest.

Simple Interest Formula


Example 1

Suppose Rohit deposits 1,000 dollar on the bank, the rate of interest per year is 0.02, and Rohit decides to deposit the money in the bank for 5 years, then the Total amount of money after 5 years will be:

  1. Identify the values to each variable: P = 1,000, r = 0.02, t = 5;
  2. Calculate using the formula: A = 1,000(1 + 0.02 * 5);

Therefore, A = 1,100 dollars.


Example 2

Suppose Jackie deposits 5,000 dollars on the bank for 50 years, and the rate of interest per year in the bank is 0.04. How much money will Jackie have in the bank 50 years later?

  1. Identify the values to each variable: P = 5,000, r = 0.04, t = 50;
  2. Calculate using the formula: A = 5,000(1 + 0.04 * 50);

Therefore, A = 15,000 dollars.
