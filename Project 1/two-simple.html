Simple Interest

Before Jumping into compound interest, we want to introduce you to simple interests first. Simple interest is easier to learn and also serves as the foundation of compound interest.

Simple Interest

Definition

Simple interest is interest calculated on the principal portion of a loan or the original contribution to a savings account. Simple interest does not compound, meaning that an account holder will only gain interest on the principal, and a borrower will never have to pay interest on interest already accrued.

Formula

Example

Suppose Rohit deposits 1,000 dollar on the bank, the rate of interest per year is 0.02, and Rohit decides to deposit the money in the bank for 5 years, then the Total amount of money after 5 years will be:

Answer:

  • P = 1,000, r = 0.02, t = 5;
  • A = 1,000(1 + 0.02 * 5)
  • A = 1,100 dollars
